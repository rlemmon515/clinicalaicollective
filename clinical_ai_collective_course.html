Four-week curriculum

Built to build.
Week by week.

Every week of AI CEA prepares you for the next. By the end of Week 4 you will have a clinical tool you built yourself and can deploy in your practice on Monday morning.

Week 1

The AI Assistant Mindset

Reframe AI as a non-clinical productivity tool. Establish the ethical foundation, understand how AI language models actually work, and write your first few-shot prompt using a four-component framework.

Deliverable: Your first structured clinical prompt and output review
Week 2

Structured Data and Triage

Transform raw clinical shorthand into formatted, discipline-specific documentation. Learn the three-category triage framework that separates observations from impressions before anything touches an AI tool.

Deliverable: Three structured prompts from real-world clinical note scenarios
Week 3

Quality Control and Clinical Voice

Audit AI-generated documentation for hallucinations, factual errors, and scope violations. Build your custom instruction set and clinical voice profile so every AI output sounds like you, not a generic template.

Deliverable: Audited documents, custom instruction set, and clinical voice profile
Week 4

Scaling the Workflow

The capstone build. Design and deploy a complete prompt library or clinical AI configuration targeting your most time-consuming weekly documentation task. Peer showcase and revision included.

Deliverable: A complete, deployable clinical tool with user guide and video walkthrough

Certificate of completion

Document your
professional development.

Upon completing the hands-on learning activities across all four weeks, you will receive a certificate documenting 10 professional development hours from the Clinical AI Collective.

SLPs may use these hours toward ASHA's professional development requirements. Acceptance for ASHA certification maintenance and state licensure renewal varies by state and individual licensure board requirements. Learners are encouraged to verify acceptance with their state board before enrollment.

OTs and COTAs may self-report hours as professional development units (PDUs) toward NBCOT recertification requirements. PTs and PTAs should verify acceptance of non-board-approved continuing education with their individual state licensing board prior to enrollment, as requirements vary by state.

Important: This certificate documents completion of 10 professional development hours. This certificate neither awards ASHA CEUs nor verifies that ASHA CEUs have been awarded. Clinical AI Collective is actively pursuing ASHA, NBCOT, and APTA continuing education provider approvals for future course offerings.
