The documentation problem

You trained to help people.
Not to write about helping people.

Documentation is consuming the hours that should belong to your clients and your life. You did not spend years earning your credential to spend evenings catching up on notes. The paperwork is not going to win anymore.

35%

of a clinician's workday spent on documentation

1 in 3

allied health professionals report burnout related to administrative burden

No AI resource built for your credential

Generic prompt packs were not written for CCC-SLP documentation standards, AOTA frameworks, or home health billing requirements. You need something built for where you actually work.

Fear of doing it wrong

HIPAA compliance, hallucination risk, and professional ethics concerns are real. You deserve clear guidance grounded in what ASHA, AOTA, and APTA actually say.

Assistant-level clinicians are overlooked

COTAs, PTAs, and SLPAs are doing the work and facing the same documentation burden. Every resource here is built for every credential in the room.

The tools exist. The training does not.

AI is already in your workplace whether you chose it or not. The question is whether you have the ethical framework and practical skills to use it on your own terms.

What we stand for

Built on three pillars.
No exceptions.

Every resource, every prompt, every course inside the Clinical AI Collective is grounded in the same non-negotiable commitments.

01

Ethical AI with Integrity

Every recommendation here reflects the official guidance of ASHA, AOTA, and APTA. The clinician is always the decision-maker. AI assists. You decide. That is not a disclaimer. That is the foundation.

02

Better Clinical Workflows

No theory without application. Every prompt, every strategy, every tool inside this community must be immediately usable in a real clinical setting by a real clinician with real documentation to finish.

03

Clinicians at the Center

This resource was not built for healthcare administrators or AI vendors. It was built for the SLP finishing notes at 9 PM, the COTA in the SNF, the PT in the home health setting. You are the center of everything here.

About the founder

Built by someone who
works as a clinician.

Regina Lemmon Bush, PhD, CCC-SLP

Regina did not start using AI because it was trendy. She started because she was sitting in a parking lot at five o'clock with a home health report unfinished, her son's preschool closing, her husband out of town, and no one available to help. Her first AI output was, by her own description, not good.

Instead of giving up, she got curious. She completed certification courses, studied peer-reviewed research, and refined her approach until she could produce a high-quality, compliant, individualized clinical draft in a fraction of the time. That process took her from parking-lot panic to serving as a clinical voice on AI workflow strategy in boardrooms.

"No allied health professional should have to figure this out alone. That is why the Clinical AI Collective exists."

PhD, Communication Sciences and Disorders CCC-SLP, ASHA Certified Full Professor Former Online Dean, Allied Healthcare Programs AI Healthcare Consultant Clinical Strategist Researcher and Grant Writer 20+ Years Clinical Practice
